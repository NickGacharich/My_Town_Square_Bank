FAQs

What is a credit score?

Your credit score is a number that represents a snapshot in time of your credit history, which lenders use to help determine how likely you are to repay a loan in the future. In a typical scoring model, your score generally ranges from a low of 300 to a high of 850. The higher the credit score, the better a borrower looks to potential lenders. There are different credit scoring models that may be used by lenders and insurers.

What credit score does Town Square Journey use?

Credit Journey uses VantageScore 3.0® by Experian™. Your lenders (including Town Square) may not use VantageScore 3.0, so the score they provide you may be different than the one you see in Credit Journey.

Does checking mt credit score lower it?

Checking your credit score on Credit Journey does not lower your credit score. We access your credit information using a soft inquiry, also known as a soft credit check, which does not impact your score.

How do I check my credit score for free?

With Town Square Credit Journey, you can check your VantageScore 3.0 credit score as often as you’d like with no impact to your credit. You can also receive your credit report directly from the three credit bureaus (Experian, TransUnion® and Equifax®) at no cost once a year.

What is a good score for credit?

Each lender is different, but generally speaking, if you have a score above 720, you'll have access to better borrowing rates. Scores above 800 are rated as exceptional.

How often should I check my free credit score?

We recommend checking your score regularly to keep track of your credit activity. With Credit Journey, we’ll let you know details of why your score changed and will send alerts when we spot new activity or changes to your credit report.

Do I need to be a Town Square customer to use Town Square Credit Journey?

No, you don’t have to be a Town Square customer to enroll.
